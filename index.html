Hardwood Flooring

Timeless, beautiful, and durable, hardwood flooring adds significant value and character to any home. It's the gold standard for a reason, offering a classic look that never goes out of style. With proper care, hardwood floors can last for generations.

Types & Finishes

  • Solid Hardwood: 3/4" thick planks of solid wood. Can be sanded and refinished multiple times.
  • Engineered Hardwood: A real wood veneer over a stable core. Ideal for areas with moisture, like basements.
  • Species: Oak (Red & White), Maple, Hickory, Walnut, Cherry, Ash.
  • Finishes: Matte, Satin (most popular), Semi-Gloss, High-Gloss.
  • Textures: Smooth, Hand-Scraped, Wire-Brushed, Distressed.

Luxury Vinyl (LVP/LVT)

Luxury Vinyl is the fastest-growing flooring category, and for good reason. It offers the stunning, realistic look of hardwood (LVP - Plank) or stone (LVT - Tile) while being 100% waterproof, highly durable, and easy to maintain. It's the perfect choice for busy homes, pets, and any room in the house.

Types & Styles

  • LVP (Luxury Vinyl Plank): Mimics hardwood planks in various widths and lengths.
  • LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile): Mimics stone, porcelain, or ceramic tile. Can even be grouted.
  • Core Types: WPC (Wood-Plastic Composite) for a softer feel, or SPC (Stone-Plastic Composite) for maximum durability and dent resistance.
  • Installation: Click-lock floating (most common) or glue-down.

Carpet

Nothing beats the soft, warm, and quiet comfort of carpet. It's an excellent choice for bedrooms, living rooms, and family spaces. Modern carpets are more stain-resistant and durable than ever, offering a vast array of colors and textures to make any space feel cozy.

Fibers & Styles

  • Nylon: The most durable and resilient fiber. Excellent for high-traffic areas.
  • Polyester (PET): Known for its exceptional softness and stain/fade resistance.
  • SmartStrand (Triexta): Combines the durability of Nylon with the stain resistance of Polyester.
  • Style: Cut Pile (Plush, Textured), Loop Pile (Berber), and Cut-and-Loop (Patterned).

Tile & Stone

For unparalleled durability and a clean, elegant look, tile and stone are the perfect choice. They are waterproof, easy to clean, and ideal for high-traffic and high-moisture areas like kitchens, bathrooms, mudrooms, and entryways. The design possibilities are virtually endless.

Materials & Styles

  • Porcelain: Dense, durable, and highly water-resistant. Can mimic wood, stone, or concrete.
  • Ceramic: A classic, cost-effective choice. Perfect for backsplashes and walls.
  • Natural Stone: Marble, Granite, Travertine, and Slate. Each piece is unique and adds luxury.
  • Styles: Large Format, Subway Tile, Mosaic, Hexagon, Patterned (Encaustic).

Laminate Flooring

Laminate offers the beautiful look of hardwood at a more budget-friendly price point. It's known for its exceptional durability, scratch resistance, and fade resistance, making it a fantastic choice for homes with pets, kids, and high-traffic areas.

Features & Styles

  • High Durability: A tough top layer (wear layer) resists scratches, dents, and stains.
  • Realistic Visuals: High-definition printing creates convincing wood, stone, or tile looks.
  • Easy Installation: Click-lock system makes for a fast and simple floating installation.
  • Water-Resistant Options: Many modern laminates now offer water-resistant or waterproof cores.

Sheet Vinyl

Sheet vinyl is a highly practical and affordable flooring solution. It comes in large rolls, allowing for a seamless or nearly seamless installation, which makes it incredibly water-resistant and easy to clean. Modern designs have come a long way, offering beautiful and realistic patterns.

Features & Styles

  • Seamless Installation: Ideal for bathrooms and laundry rooms where water is a concern.
  • Cost-Effective: One of the most affordable flooring options available.
  • Comfortable: Often has a cushioned backing, making it softer and warmer underfoot than tile.
  • Styles: Realistic wood-look, stone-look, and modern geometric patterns.

Cork Flooring

Cork is a fantastic eco-friendly flooring choice. Harvested from the bark of cork oak trees, it's a sustainable and renewable resource. It's naturally soft, quiet, and warm, providing excellent thermal and acoustic insulation. It's also naturally hypoallergenic and anti-microbial.

Benefits & Styles

  • Eco-Friendly: A truly sustainable and biodegradable material.
  • Comfort: Soft and cushioned underfoot, making it great for standing.
  • Quiet & Warm: Natural insulation reduces noise and keeps floors warm.
  • Styles: Available in planks or tiles with natural textures or modern, stained colors.

Hardwood Refinishing

Do your hardwood floors look scratched, dull, or outdated? Don't replace themrefinish them! Our professional refinishing service can restore your old floors to their original beauty, or give them a completely new look with a different stain.

Our Process

  • Dustless Sanding: We use modern equipment to sand your floors down to the raw wood while minimizing airborne dust.
  • Stain Application: Choose from a wide variety of stain colors to match your new decor, or keep it natural.
  • Protective Finish: We apply multiple coats of durable polyurethane (in matte, satin, or gloss) to protect your floors for years.
  • Buff & Recoat: For floors with light scratches, a "buff and recoat" can refresh the top layer without a full sanding.

Bamboo Flooring

Bamboo is a popular eco-friendly alternative to hardwood. As a fast-growing grass, it's a highly renewable resource. It offers a clean, modern aesthetic and, depending on the type, can be even harder and more durable than traditional oak.

Types & Styles

  • Solid (Vertical/Horizontal): Traditional bamboo looks. Horizontal shows the "knuckles" of the grass, while Vertical is more linear.
  • Strand-Woven: Bamboo fibers are compressed with resin to create an incredibly dense and durable plank, often twice as hard as oak.
  • Engineered Bamboo: A bamboo veneer over a stable core, similar to engineered hardwood.
  • Colors: Typically available in "Natural" (light blonde) or "Carbonized" (a darker, caramel tone).

Rubber Flooring

Rubber flooring is the ultimate choice for durability, shock absorption, and slip resistance. While traditionally used in commercial gyms, it's becoming a popular choice for home gyms, playrooms, workshops, and laundry rooms. It's incredibly tough, easy to clean, and provides excellent cushioning.

Types & Formats

  • Interlocking Tiles: The most popular for home gyms. Easy to install and reconfigure.
  • Rolls: Provides a seamless, professional look. Best for large, open areas.
  • Square Tiles: Glue-down tiles for a permanent, heavy-duty installation.
  • Finishes: Typically available in solid black, or black with colored flecks (e.g., blue, grey, red).
